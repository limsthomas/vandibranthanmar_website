MACHINES ARE MY BREATH

How it all Started

In my childhood i use to live in my mothers house. It was at vallakkunnu, a place in Thrissur district in Kerala. Our house was on the main road side where numerous vehicles pass by daily. These vehicles includes those public transport vehicles like, busses ; goods transport vehicles like lorries, tempos, pickups ; and other private vehicles including wide variety of two and four wheelers.

The huge vehicles were my favourites, and i used to remember the names and timings of the busses passing through that road daily. I enjoyed the weekends to the most, just by sitting in front of the house and clapping hands whenever a vehicle passes by. Also i use to checkout for the fastest bus. In kallettumkara, a place nearby vallakkunnu, there was a railway cross. At the time of gate closing, there won't be any vehicles on the road, except the ones moving towards the opposite side.

At that time, we can merge into a pin drop silence, where we can hear the sound of the train, moving kilometres away. The rush of the busses and vehicles after the opening of the railwaygate use to give me a serious adrenaline rush.The sounds of their honking, overtaking, gear downing etc still boosts my mind.Those giant machines have stolen my mind forever.

In the front of our house, there was a pair of beams whose base was circular in shape; which was my first steering. The steering was accompanied by a seating space. And it was one day that i saw a bus from a driving institute passing by, i screamed to them and gave a nice high five, which i got back.

At that instant i planned in my mind, of taking a heavy licens when it will be time. And i kept my promise.
